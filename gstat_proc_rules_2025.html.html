CHAPTER I — PRELIMINARY
Rules 1-2 — Short Title, Commencement & Definitions ⇧ Index
⚖️ GSTAT Procedure Rules 2025
G.S.R. 256(E) | Notified 24th April 2025 | u/s 111 CGST Act 2017
📝 The GST Appellate Tribunal (GSTAT) has finally been established and these rules govern how appeals before it will be filed, heard and decided. For every GST practitioner, these rules are the new Code of Practice at the apex GST forum.
RULE 1 Short Title: "Goods and Services Tax Appellate Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 2025"
Commencement: Date of publication in Official Gazette (24th April 2025)
Rule 2 — Key Definitions (Selected Critical Ones)
"Act"
CGST Act 2017 OR concerned State GST Act OR UT GST Act
📝 The rules apply uniformly across CGST, SGST and UTGST — one set of rules governs all three.
"Authorised Representative"
(i) Govt. appointed officer for Commissioner
(ii) Person authorised in writing / vakalatnama (duly stamped) u/s 116 of Act
📝 A CA/CMA/Advocate can appear as authorised representative. Vakalatnama must be duly stamped — a plain paper authorisation is NOT enough.
💡 Practical: Ensure your vakalatnama is stamped as per the respective High Court rules of your State — unstamped vakalatnama = defective filing.
"Certified Copy"
Original order received by party OR copy authenticated by department OR copy authenticated by AR of appellant/respondent
"GSTAT Portal"
Web portal specified by order of President for functioning of Tribunal
📝 Everything is online — filing, service of notices, scrutiny, hearing. The GSTAT Portal is the single window for all proceedings.
"Interlocutory Application"
Application in any pending appeal/proceeding — other than execution of order
📝 Stay application, condonation of delay, early hearing — all are interlocutory applications. Fee: ₹5,000 per application.
"Principal Bench"
Constituted under s.109(3) CGST Act | vs | "State Bench" under s.109(4)
CHAPTER II — POWERS AND FUNCTIONS
Rules 3-6 — Time Computation, Order Format, Seal & Custody of Records ⇧ Index
⏱️ Administrative Framework — Rules 3 to 6
RULE 3 — Time Computation
Day from which period is to be reckoned → EXCLUDED
If last day falls on a closed day → that day + all succeeding closed days → EXCLUDED
📝 Standard exclusion rule — same as civil courts. If limitation expires on a Sunday or holiday, next working day is the last day.
💡 Appeal filing deadline expires on 15th Aug (holiday) → 16th Aug is the valid last date, no condonation needed.
RULE 4 — Format of Orders/Directions
Every ruling/direction/order/summons issued in name of President or Member
Signed by Registrar (or authorised officer)
Sealed with official seal of Tribunal (for physical copy)
📝 An order without the Registrar's signature and official seal is not a valid order — it cannot be enforced or appealed against.
RULE 5 — Official Seal
Seal/emblem → as President may specify from time to time
Custody: with Registrar
RULE 6 — Custody of Records
Registrar has custody of all records
No record allowed outside Tribunal custody WITHOUT leave of Tribunal
Proviso: Registrar may allow officer to remove record for administrative purposes
📝 Once a document is filed, it cannot be taken back without Tribunal's permission. AR cannot casually retrieve filed documents.
Rules 7-9 — Sittings, Hours & Working Hours ⇧ Index
🕐 Sittings & Working Hours of GSTAT
RULE 7 — Location of Benches
Bench holds sittings at locations notified by Central Government
RULE 8 — Sitting Hours
Ordinarily: 10:30 AM – 1:30 PM AND 2:30 PM – 4:30 PM
Subject to President's order
Tribunal may extend sitting as it deems fit
💡 If your matter is urgent, the Tribunal can sit beyond 4:30 PM — request can be made for extended hearing.
RULE 9 — Office Hours
Administrative office: 9:30 AM – 6:00 PM on all working days
Subject to President's order
📝 Registry opens at 9:30 — you can file documents even before the court starts at 10:30. Useful for urgent filings.
Rules 10-14 — Inherent Powers, Calendar, Listing & Extension of Time ⇧ Index
⚡ Key Procedural Powers — Rules 10 to 14
RULE 10 — INHERENT POWERS
Nothing in these rules limits the Tribunal's inherent powers to:
• Make orders necessary for ends of justice
• Prevent abuse of process
📝 Inherent powers are residual powers not curtailed by any specific rule. Even if no rule covers a situation, the Tribunal can act to prevent injustice. This is a powerful tool for practitioners.
💡 If Department is fishing for documents to create a new demand, argue abuse of process under Rule 10 to restrain such summons.
RULE 11 — Calendar
Working days calendar → decided by President and Members
RULE 12 — Listing of Urgent Matters
Urgent matter filed BEFORE 12 NOON → listed next working day (if complete)
Exceptional: Filed after 12 noon but BEFORE 3 PM → next day, with SPECIFIC PERMISSION of Tribunal/President
📝 Cut-off time: 12 noon for urgent listing. If you miss the 12 noon cut-off, you need special permission. Always aim to file urgent matters by 11:30 AM.
RULE 13 — Power to Exempt
On sufficient cause shown → Tribunal may exempt parties from compliance with any rule
May give directions for substantial justice
💡 If your appeal has a minor defect (e.g., document not indexed) — apply for exemption from strict compliance under Rule 13 rather than letting the appeal be rejected.
RULE 14 — Power to Extend Time
Tribunal may extend any time fixed under rules or by order
Extension can be ordered EVEN AFTER time has expired
📝 Unlike limitation under the Act, procedural time under these Rules can be extended even after expiry. The Tribunal has this discretion in the interest of justice.
Rules 15-17 — Registrar's Powers, Adjournment & Delegation ⇧ Index
📋 Registrar & Delegation — Rules 15 to 17
Rule 15 — Functions of the Registrar (10 functions)
Day-to-day administration
Notify filing procedure
Registration & scrutiny of appeals/applications
Receive amendment applications
Summons & service matters
Inspection of documents
Maintain records & manage registry
RULE 16 — Adjournment
Normally sought BEFORE Bench
Extraordinary circumstances: Registrar may (if directed by Tribunal in chambers) adjourn and place before Tribunal in chambers
📝 Adjournment is the Bench's call — Registrar can only do it in exceptional cases when directed by the Tribunal itself (not as a matter of course).
RULE 17 — Delegation by President
(1) President may delegate functions to Vice-President of State Bench
(2) President may delegate Registrar's functions to Joint/Deputy/Assistant Registrar or other suitable officer
CHAPTER III — INSTITUTION OF APPEALS
Rules 18-22 — Filing Appeals: Format, Contents, Documents & Verification ⇧ Index
📁 Filing of Appeals before GSTAT — Rules 18 to 22
Rule 18 — Filing of Appeals
MODE
Filed ONLINE on GSTAT Portal in prescribed Form
📝 No physical filing — everything is on GSTAT Portal. Ensure you have login credentials well before the deadline.
CAUSE TITLE
"In the Goods and Service Tax Appellate Tribunal" + set out proceedings/order against which preferred
STRUCTURE
Divided into numbered paragraphs
Each paragraph — one separate fact/allegation/point
GSTIN, full names, addresses at beginning
💡 Avoid mixing multiple grounds in one paragraph — Tribunal may ignore unparagraphed grounds.
PARTIES
Numbered consecutively; separate line per party; numbers NOT changed during proceedings
If party dies during appeal → legal heirs/representatives shown as sub-numbers
RULE 18(2) — ONE APPEAL RULE
Regardless of number of SCNs / refund claims / demands dealt with in the impugned order → ONE appeal suffices against one order
📝 Even if the order-in-appeal covers 10 different SCNs — you file ONE combined appeal. No need to file 10 separate appeals.
RULE 18(3) — MULTIPLE OIOs
(a) If impugned order-in-appeal covers multiple Orders-in-Original → Form of appeal to be as many as the number of OIOs
(b) If impugned order relates to multiple persons → EACH aggrieved person files SEPARATE appeal — NO joint/common appeals
📝 Joint appeals are not allowed. Even if the order covers two group companies, each must file separately. This is a critical requirement — joint appeal will not be entertained.
💡 M/s A and M/s B (related entities) receive one common order → M/s A files appeal no. 1, M/s B files appeal no. 2 — they cannot file a single joint appeal.
Rules 19-22 — Other Filing Requirements
R.19 — Date of Presentation
Registrar endorses date of presentation on Form of appeal (if manual filing)
R.20 — Contents
Grounds: concise, distinct heads, numbered consecutively, double space on A4
All forms/COs/applications: neatly typed, double space, A4, paged, indexed, tagged in separate folder
💡 Presentation matters! An untidy, unpaged filing can be returned by Registrar. Invest in proper formatting.
R.21 — Documents
Certified copy of impugned order (mandatory)
Where order passed in appeal/revision → certified copy of revisional order + original order + all relied upon documents
Fee as per Rule 110(5) CGST Rules submitted online → Final acknowledgement from GSTAT Portal
📝 No certified copy = defective filing. Registrar can return the appeal for rectification. The Portal issues the final acknowledgement only after fee payment and document upload.
R.22 — Endorsement
AR's name + signature at foot of every appeal/pleading
Every appeal/pleading signed and verified by party concerned
Rules 23-28 — Translation, Scrutiny, Defects, Registration & Authorization ⇧ Index
📄 Scrutiny, Defects & Registration — Rules 23 to 28
R.23 — Translation
Non-English documents → must be accompanied by certified English translation (agreed by both parties OR certified by AR)
Appeal NOT set down for hearing until ALL documents confirmed in English / translated
📝 All documents before GSTAT must be in English. If your State orders are in Marathi/Hindi/Kannada — get certified translation done before filing.
R.24 — Defective Documents
If defective → returned to party with notice
Failure to comply within 7 working days → placed before Registrar for orders
Registrar may allow rectification time up to max 30 days from filing
If no steps → Registrar may DECLINE to register (reasons in writing)
After personal hearing, if unsatisfied → listed before Bench with defects → Bench may accept OR reject
📝 The 7-day window is critical — if you miss it, the appeal may be declined. Always check for defects before filing and respond immediately to defect notices.
💡 Registrar returns your appeal with defect on 1 June → You must rectify and refile by 8 June (7 working days). Missing this → the appeal goes before Registrar for decline order.
R.25 — Registration
On admission → numbered and registered in appropriate register → number entered (Index modified)
R.26 — Ex-Parte Amendments
Arithmetical/grammatical/clerical errors → rectified on Registrar's order WITHOUT notice to parties
Proviso: No ex-parte amendments AFTER respondent appears
R.27 — Calling of Records
On admission → Registrar (if directed by Tribunal) calls records from concerned Bench/adjudicating authority → retransmits at conclusion
R.28 — Authorization
Person filing on behalf of party → must produce TRUE COPY of authorization letter for verification by Registrar
Registrar may call for further materials at any time
📝 If you are filing on behalf of a company or individual — always have the authorization letter ready. Board Resolution / Power of Attorney / signed authorization letter is required.
Rules 29-37 — IA, Grounds, Respondents, Cross-Objections & Reply ⇧ Index
📑 Interlocutory Applications, Grounds & Respondents — Rules 29 to 37
R.29 — Interlocutory Application
For: stay / direction / rectification / condonation of delay / early hearing / exemption from certified copy / extension
Must include all info in GSTAT FORM-01
Must be supported by AFFIDAVIT
📝 Every IA needs an affidavit — a bare application without affidavit is defective. For stay applications, the affidavit must state facts establishing prima facie case and irreparable hardship.
💡 Stay application: File GSTAT FORM-01 + affidavit stating (a) prima facie case, (b) balance of convenience, (c) irreparable injury — same principles as High Court stay.
R.31 — Grounds in Appeal
Appellant CANNOT urge grounds NOT in appeal Form — EXCEPT by leave of Tribunal
But: Tribunal NOT confined to grounds in Form when deciding
Proviso: Tribunal cannot decide on NEW ground without giving affected party opportunity to be heard
📝 Put ALL grounds in your appeal Form — you cannot raise new grounds later without leave. The Tribunal however has wider power to decide on any ground, but must give you hearing on it.
R.32 — Rejection / Amendment
Registrar MAY (sufficient cause) accept Form even without documents in R.21 → but requires appellant to file docs/amendments within time (MAX 30 days)
Registrar may REJECT if docs not produced/amendments not made within time
R.33 — Respondents
Appeal by person other than Commissioner → Commissioner is Respondent
Appeal by Commissioner → other party is Respondent
R.34 — Copies to Respondent
Copy of appeal + documents → provided to respondent AND concerned Commissioner as soon as filed
R.35 — Cross-Objections
Cross-objections / applications → registered and numbered → appeal rules apply mutatis mutandis
R.36 — Reply by Respondent
Reply within ONE MONTH of receipt
Copy of reply + documents served on applicant by respondent
Applicant shall specifically admit/deny/rebut respondent's facts + state additional facts
📝 Respondent has one month to file reply. If Department doesn't file reply in time — proceed without it. As appellant, on receipt of reply, you must specifically admit or deny each fact.
R.37 — Rejoinder
If respondent states additional facts → Bench MAY allow petitioner to file rejoinder (on GSTAT Portal)
Advance copy to respondent within ONE MONTH or extended time
CHAPTER IV — CAUSE LIST
Rules 38-40 — Cause List, Notices & Service ⇧ Index
📋 Cause List & Service of Notices — Rules 38 to 40
Rule 38 — Daily Cause List
Published on: Notice Board + GSTAT Portal — before closing hours each working day (GSTAT CDR-01)
Priority Order in Cause List (Rule 38(2))
① Pronouncement of orders
② Clarification
③ Admission
④ Orders/Directions
⑤ Part-heard (latest first)
⑥ Numerical order / as directed
R.39 — Holiday/Cancellation
If Tribunal does not sit (holiday/unforeseen reason) → new cause list prepared for listed cases
If Bench cancelled (Member unable) → Registrar adjourns to convenient date
Adjournment notified on Notice Board + GSTAT Portal
R.40 — Service of Notices
Service as per Section 169 of the Act (GSTAT Portal = "common portal" for this purpose)
Tribunal may direct substituted service if normal service not practicable
Service on AR = deemed proper service on party
📝 Notice served on your AR (CA/Advocate on record) = notice served on you. Ensure your AR is reachable and monitors the Portal — you cannot claim non-receipt if served on AR.
💡 Register on GSTAT Portal → enable email/SMS notifications → never miss a notice or hearing date.
CHAPTER V — HEARING OF APPEAL
Rules 41-46 — Hearing Procedure, Default, Ex-Parte & Evidence ⇧ Index
🎤 Hearing of Appeals — Rules 41 to 46
R.41 — PROCEDURE
Appellant heard first in support of appeal
Respondent heard against appeal (if necessary)
Appellant entitled to REPLY after respondent's arguments
💡 Always exercise the right of reply — this is your opportunity to answer the Department's arguments without them getting a further chance to respond.
R.42 — DEFAULT BY APPELLANT
If appellant absent → Tribunal may:
(a) DISMISS for default, OR
(b) Hear and decide on MERITS
Proviso: If dismissed for default → appellant appears later with sufficient cause → Tribunal SHALL restore the appeal
📝 Dismissal for default is not the end — you can get the appeal restored by showing sufficient cause for non-appearance. File restoration application promptly.
R.43 — EX-PARTE
If appellant present but respondent absent → Tribunal may HEAR AND DECIDE EX-PARTE
📝 Department does not appear → you can proceed ex-parte. Get an ex-parte order in your favour — this is a powerful remedy when Department repeatedly fails to appear.
R.44 — DEATH/INSOLVENCY
If appellant/respondent dies / adjudicated insolvent / company wound up → appeal ABATES
UNLESS application for continuance by successor-in-interest / executor / liquidator within 60 days of event
Further time: if prevented by sufficient cause, Tribunal may extend
📝 60-day window to apply for substitution of legal heirs. Miss this → appeal abates. File substitution application immediately on learning of party's death.
Rules 45-46 — Additional Evidence & Affidavit Evidence
R.45 — ADDITIONAL EVIDENCE
Parties NOT entitled to produce additional evidence (oral or documentary) before GSTAT as a right
BUT Tribunal MAY allow (reasons in writing) if:
• Documents/witnesses necessary to pass orders
• Any sufficient cause
• Adjudicating/appellate authority decided without giving sufficient opportunity to adduce evidence
📝 You cannot produce new documents as a matter of right at GSTAT. But if the lower authority denied you a fair hearing, GSTAT can allow additional evidence. This is a crucial safeguard against arbitrary orders.
💡 If AO/AA rejected your reply without reading it → argue before GSTAT for leave to adduce additional evidence as the lower authority denied sufficient opportunity.
R.46 — AFFIDAVIT EVIDENCE
Tribunal may direct evidence by affidavit
If natural justice requires → Tribunal may order CROSS-EXAMINATION of deponent (via video conferencing or otherwise) on application by any party
📝 If the other side files an affidavit making false claims — apply for cross-examination under Rule 46(2). This is available at GSTAT unlike lower forums.
Rules 47-52 — Adjournment, Open Proceedings, Larger Bench & Orders ⇧ Index
📜 Orders & Larger Bench — Rules 47 to 52
R.47 — Adjournment
Tribunal may adjourn at any stage on such terms as it deems fit
R.48 — Open Proceedings
Proceedings OPEN to public
Proviso: Tribunal may order in camera proceedings — excluding public/specific persons from room/building
📝 GSTAT proceedings are public by default — like High Court. This means orders become publicly available, which is important for precedent setting.
R.50 — LARGER BENCH
Different opinion of Members of Bench → appeal referred to LARGER BENCH by President
📝 If two members of the Bench disagree → the matter goes to a larger Bench for decision. This is how binding precedents on contested issues will be settled at GSTAT.
💡 Strategic: If you have a complex issue where current members seem divided — the matter may get referred to a larger Bench creating more authoritative precedent.
Rule 51 — Order Signing & Dating (Critical for Limitation Purposes)
Order dictated in open court → date of dictation = date of final order
Order reserved → date of pronouncement = date of final order
Gist pronounced first, detailed order later → date all Members sign = date of order (last signature date controls)
📝 The date of the order determines when your 90-day limitation for High Court challenge begins. Watch for the date carefully — it is NOT always the date of hearing.
R.52 — Publication of Orders
Fit orders may be released for publication in authoritative reports / press on terms laid by Tribunal
CHAPTER VI — RECORD OF PROCEEDINGS
Rules 53-58 — Court Diary, Order Sheet & Regulation of Court Work ⇧ Index
📒 Record of Proceedings — Rules 53 to 58
R.53 — Court Diary (GSTAT CDR-02)
Maintained by Court Officer
Contains: history of appeal, substance of orders, execution record
Checked by Dy./Asst. Registrar — initialled once a fortnight
R.54 — Order Sheet (GSTAT FORM-02)
Contains ALL orders from time to time
All orders in English; signed by Members
Proviso: Routine orders (call for records, adjournment) signed by Court Officer
R.55 — Court Diary (GSTAT Portal)
Court Officer maintains diary on GSTAT Portal recording proceedings for each sitting
Records: adjourned / partly heard / heard and disposed / heard and reserved + next dates
R.56 — Citations for Reference
Parties/AR/legal practitioners → before commencement of proceedings for the day → furnish to Court Officer: list of law journals, reports, statutes, citations (or photocopy of full text)
📝 File your citations list before the day's proceedings start — not during the hearing. This allows the Bench to review them in advance. Always carry full photocopies of judgments you intend to cite.
R.57 — Calling Cases
Court Officer calls cases in serial order (subject to Bench orders)

R.58 — Court Regulation
Dy./Asst. Registrar ensures no inconvenience to Bench (staff, stenographer)
Court Officer ensures silence, dignity and decorum in and around Court Hall
Orders/directions → immediately transmitted to Dy./Asst. Registrar → verified → communicated

CHAPTER VIII — INSPECTION OF RECORDS
Rules 67-71 — Inspection of Records: Application, Mode & Register ⇧ Index
🔍 Inspection of Records — Rules 67 to 71
R.67 — Who Can Inspect
Applicant to the case OR their AR
Mode: Written application in GSTAT FORM-03 to Registrar + prescribed fee (₹5,000)
R.68 — Grant of Inspection
Only on order of Registrar (for pending or decided cases)
R.69 — Application Timing
Between 10:30 AM – 1:30 PM on working days
2 days BEFORE date of inspection sought
Inspection NOT ordinarily permitted on date of hearing OR preceding day
📝 Apply for inspection at least 2 working days before you need it. If your hearing is on Friday — apply by Wednesday at the latest.
R.70 — MODE OF INSPECTION
Times: 10:30-12:30 AND 2:30-4:30 PM in presence of authorised officer
Person inspecting:
✗ Cannot cause dislocation/mutilation/tampering/damage
✗ Cannot make any marking on records
Supervising officer may PROHIBIT further inspection for violation → report to Registrar
📝 Inspection is strictly supervised — you cannot take documents out, mark them or damage them. Take notes carefully. Photography is not specifically permitted — ask Registrar's permission.
R.71 — Register of Inspection (GSTAT CDR-06)
Dy./Asst. Registrar maintains inspection register
Person inspecting signs on register AND on application at conclusion of inspection
CHAPTER IX — APPEARANCE OF AUTHORISED REPRESENTATIVES
Rules 72-77 — AR Appearance, Vakalatnama, Restrictions & Dress ⇧ Index
👔 Authorised Representatives Before GSTAT — Rules 72 to 77
R.72 — VAKALATNAMA MANDATORY
No legal practitioner/AR entitled to appear in any proceeding UNLESS vakalatnama / Memorandum of Appearance / Letter of Authorisation (GSTAT FORM-04) filed
Duly executed by/on behalf of party
📝 You MUST file vakalatnama before the very first appearance. Appearing without filed vakalatnama = not entitled to be heard. File it during/before admission stage.
R.73 — CHANGE OF AR
Proposing to file vakalatnama in pending case where another AR is already on record → only with:
(a) Written consent of AR on record, OR
(b) Permission of Tribunal after revocation of existing vakalatnama (on application served on existing counsel)
Exception: No consent needed for application u/s 112(3) (Commissioner's appeal)
📝 If you want to take over a case from another CA/Advocate — get their NOC (No Objection Certificate) first. Without NOC, you cannot appear. Client cannot simply change AR without the existing AR's consent.
💡 If original CA refuses to give NOC → file application before Tribunal for permission to revoke existing vakalatnama after serving notice on the old CA.
R.74 — CONFLICT OF INTEREST
AR who has:
• Given advice in institution of case
• Drawn pleadings for a party
• Acted for a party during proceedings
→ CANNOT appear for any person whose interest is OPPOSED to former client
EXCEPT with prior permission of Tribunal
📝 Classic conflict of interest rule. If you advised M/s A against M/s B and now M/s B wants to engage you in the same matter — you cannot appear without Tribunal's permission. Professional ethics apply.
R.75 — Party's Right Restricted
Party who has engaged AR may be RESTRICTED by Tribunal from making direct presentation
R.76 — Special Panel
Tribunal may draw up panel of ARs / valuers / experts for assisting in proceedings
Remuneration specified in consultation with Tribunal
R.77 — PROFESSIONAL DRESS
AR shall wear same professional dress as prescribed in their Code of Conduct

For others (Rule 122):
Male: Close-collared black coat OR open-collared black coat, white shirt, black tie
Female: Black coat over white sari or other white dress
Summer (15 April – 31 August): Black coat may be dispensed with
Note: Regular employee of a party ≠ Departmental officer appointed as AR (departmental officer must wear professional dress)
💡 CAs and CMAs must wear professional dress (as per ICAI/ICMAI Code). Advocates wear black coat and gown as usual. A company's internal officer appearing as AR must wear black coat + white shirt + black tie — not casual clothes.
CHAPTER X — AFFIDAVITS
Rules 78-83 — Affidavits: Form, Attestation & Annexures ⇧ Index
📜 Affidavits Before GSTAT — Rules 78 to 83
R.78 — Title
"Before the Goods and Services Tax Appellate Tribunal (GSTAT)" followed by cause title
R.79 — Form
Conforms to Order XIX, Rule 3 of Civil Procedure Code, 1908
📝 CPC Order XIX Rule 3: Affidavit must be confined to facts within deponent's personal knowledge (or belief, with source stated). Hearsay affidavits can be challenged.
R.80 — Attestation
Sworn/affirmed BEFORE: Advocate OR Notary (who shall affix official seal)
📝 Affidavit before a mere oath commissioner is NOT valid — it must be before an Advocate or Notary with their official seal.
R.81 — Illiterate / Visually Challenged
Attester must certify that: affidavit was read/explained/translated to deponent → deponent understood it → signed/marked in attester's presence (GSTAT FORM-05)
R.82 — Identification
If deponent not known to attester → identity testified by person known to attester → identifying person affixes signature
R.83 — Annexures
Documents attached = referred to as "Annexure [number]"
Attester endorses each annexure with the number and their name/designation/signature
💡 Always number annexures properly in your affidavits — Annexure-A, Annexure-B, etc. Unnumbered or mis-numbered annexures can be rejected by the Bench.
CHAPTER XI — DISCOVERY, PRODUCTION AND RETURN OF DOCUMENTS
Rules 84-87 — Summons for Documents, Suo Motu, Marking & Return ⇧ Index
📂 Discovery & Production of Documents — Rules 84 to 87
R.84 — Summons for Documents
Governed by CPC provisions (otherwise)
Application on plain paper → state: document sought, relevancy, whether certified copy would serve purpose and result of any prior application
Summons to public officer → in GSTAT FORM-06 → addressed to Head of Department
📝 If you need documents from a government department (DGGI, DGSBI, customs) — file application for summons under Rule 84. State clearly why the document is relevant and why certified copy will not suffice.
💡 Want original audit report from GST Audit wing? Apply under Rule 84 — GSTAT can summon those records compulsorily.
R.85 — Suo Motu Power
Tribunal may suo motu summon public or other documents from public officer's custody — notwithstanding anything in these rules
📝 GSTAT can call for records on its own — without any party applying. This is significant — the Tribunal can independently investigate matters if it finds something amiss.
R.86 — Marking of Documents
'A' Series → Appellant/Petitioner's documents
'B' Series → Respondent's documents
'C' Series → Tribunal exhibits
Tribunal may direct deposit for expenses of record transmission

⚖️ Disposal & Pronouncement of Orders — Rules 99 to 114
R.99 — Disposal
After reasonable opportunity to be heard → Tribunal passes such orders as it thinks fit
Proviso: Tribunal may summarily dismiss appeal if no sufficient grounds (reasons in writing)
📝 Tribunal can dismiss an appeal at the admission stage itself — without full hearing — if no prima facie case exists. Ensure your grounds are strong enough to survive admission.
R.100 — Operative Portion
All orders/directions stated in clear and precise terms in LAST PARAGRAPH of order
💡 When analyzing a GSTAT order for enforcement or challenge — always jump to the last paragraph for the operative portion (what was actually ordered/directed).
R.102 — Costs
Tribunal may in its discretion impose costs on defaulting party as it deems fit
📝 Frivolous appeals or unreasonable conduct → risk of costs being imposed. Conversely, if Department files unreasonable appeal → you can request costs against Department.
R.103 — PRONOUNCEMENT TIMELINE
Order pronounced at once OR as soon as practicable BUT NOT LATER THAN 30 DAYS from final hearing (excluding vacations/holidays)
Every order in writing, signed and dated by all Members
Certified copy given to parties
Tribunal may transmit order to court for enforcement
📝 30-day outer limit for pronouncing orders after final hearing. If order is not pronounced within 30 days — you can write to Registrar/President requesting expeditious pronouncement.
💡 Track your hearing date + 30 days. If no order by then → politely remind the Registry in writing. This prevents indefinite reservation of orders.

R.104 — Any Member May Pronounce
Any Member of Bench may pronounce order for/on behalf of entire Bench
Court officer records this in order sheet
R.105 — Authorized Pronouncement
If Members not available / ceased to be Members → President may authorise another Member to pronounce (after verifying order is signed by all)
If any Member cannot sign (death/retirement/resignation) → deemed part-heard → listed afresh
R.106 — RECUSAL
Mandatory recusal when:
(a) Personal/familial/professional relationship with party
(b) Previously advised/represented/acted as expert/witness in same matter
(c) Other circumstances making participation seem inappropriate
Proviso: NO party has right to KNOW the reasons for recusal
📝 Recusal is the Member's own decision — parties cannot force a recusal (unlike CJI/SC practice). However, you can bring the relationship to the Member's attention through proper application, and they may voluntarily recuse.
💡 If a GSTAT Member is the former partner of your firm, or previously appeared as advocate in your matter — point this out discreetly at the first date — the Member will likely recuse.

R.108 — Rectification of Order
Clerical mistakes / accidental slips / omissions → corrected by Tribunal suo motu or on application
Application: Online (GSTAT FORM-01) within ONE MONTH from date of final order
📝 If the GSTAT order contains a date error, wrong amount, or clerical mistake — apply for rectification within 1 month. Beyond 1 month you lose this right.

Within 30 DAYS from completion of pleadings → Tribunal may amend any defect/error in proceedings on terms as to costs

⚔️ Hearing Arsenal — GSTAT Procedure Rules 2025 ⇧ Index
Practical arguments and procedural weapons drawn from the Rules — for use in GSTAT hearings, objections and applications
Rule 10 — Inherent Powers of GSTAT
Goods and Services Tax Appellate Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 2025
Key Principle
Nothing in these rules shall be deemed to limit or otherwise affect the inherent powers of the Appellate Tribunal to make such orders or give such directions as may be necessary for meeting the ends of justice or to prevent abuse of the process of the Appellate Tribunal.
When to Use — When Department's summons/notice or action appears to be a fishing expedition or is an abuse of process. Invoke Rule 10 before the Bench to restrain such conduct. Also use when no specific rule covers the relief you seek — argue that inherent powers fill the gap. Available even at admission stage.
Rule 13 — Power to Exempt from Compliance
Goods and Services Tax Appellate Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 2025
Key Principle
The Appellate Tribunal may on sufficient cause being shown, exempt the parties from compliance with any requirement of these rules and may give such directions in matters of practice and procedure, as it may consider just and expedient on the application moved in this behalf to render substantial justice.
When to Use — When your filing has a procedural defect (insufficient copies, indexing error, minor format issue) and Registrar threatens to return/reject. Argue for exemption from strict compliance under Rule 13. The test is "sufficient cause" — show that the defect does not prejudice the other side and that rejecting would deny substantial justice.
Rule 14 — Power to Extend Time (Even After Expiry)
Goods and Services Tax Appellate Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 2025
Key Principle
The Appellate Tribunal may extend the time appointed by these rules or fixed by any order, for doing any act or taking any proceeding, upon such terms, if any, as the justice of the case may require, and any extension may be ordered, although the application for the same is not made until the expiration of the time appointed or allowed.
When to Use — When a procedural deadline under these Rules (e.g., filing reply, filing rejoinder, rectification application time) has expired. Unlike limitation under the Act, procedural time can be extended even after expiry. Distinguish this from the statutory limitation for filing the appeal itself (which cannot be extended under these rules).
Rule 18(3)(b) — No Joint Appeals Permitted
Goods and Services Tax Appellate Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 2025
Key Principle
In case an impugned order is in respect of more than one person, each aggrieved person will be required to file a separate appeal, and common appeals or joint appeals shall not be entertained.
When to Use — (a) Defensive: If Department has filed a common appeal covering multiple assessees — argue that it is not maintainable under Rule 18(3)(b) and must be separately filed. (b) Offensive: If your co-noticee has filed a joint appeal with you — ensure you have your own separate appeal to avoid your case being impacted by the other party's conduct.
Rule 31 — Grounds NOT in Form Require Leave
Goods and Services Tax Appellate Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 2025
Key Principle
The appellant shall not, except by leave of the Appellate Tribunal, urge or be heard in support of any grounds not set forth in the Form of appeal, but the Appellate Tribunal, in deciding the appeal, shall not be confined to the grounds set forth in the Form... Provided that the Appellate Tribunal shall not rest its decision on any other grounds unless the party who may be affected thereby has had a sufficient opportunity of being heard on that ground.
When to Use — (Defensive against Department): If Revenue attempts to argue new grounds not raised in the impugned order or in their appeal Form — object that they need leave of Tribunal under Rule 31 and that you must be given opportunity to be heard. (Offensive as appellant): Apply for leave to urge additional grounds discovered during appeal proceedings.
Rule 42 — Dismissal for Default is Restorable
Goods and Services Tax Appellate Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 2025
Key Principle
Where an appeal has been dismissed for default and the appellant appears afterwards and satisfies the Appellate Tribunal that there was sufficient cause for his non-appearance when the appeal was called on for hearing, the Appellate Tribunal shall make an order setting aside the dismissal and restore the appeal.
When to Use — If your appeal has been dismissed for non-appearance due to medical emergency, travel disruption, connectivity failure (for online hearings), or other sufficient cause — file restoration application immediately under Rule 42 proviso. The word "shall" in the rule is mandatory — Tribunal must restore if sufficient cause is shown. Attach supporting evidence (medical certificate, travel record, etc.).
Rule 45 — Additional Evidence: Denied Opportunity Below = Right at GSTAT
Goods and Services Tax Appellate Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 2025
Key Principle
The parties to the appeal shall not be entitled to produce any additional evidence... Provided that... if adjudicating authority or the appellate or revisional authority has decided the case without giving sufficient opportunity to any party to adduce evidence on the points specified by them or not specified by them, the Appellate Tribunal may, for reasons to be recorded, allow such documents to be produced... or such evidence to be adduced.
When to Use — Where AO issued SCN and passed order on same day without giving time to respond, or where AO passed order ignoring reply, or where AA decided ex-parte without proper notice — argue before GSTAT for leave to adduce additional evidence under Rule 45 proviso. This is a constitutional safeguard — natural justice violations below = right to additional evidence above.
Rule 50 — Reference to Larger Bench for Conflicting Views
Goods and Services Tax Appellate Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 2025
Key Principle
In case of different opinion of Members of Bench while hearing an appeal, the appeal shall be referred to larger Bench by the President, as it deems fit, for disposal of the appeal.
When to Use — When Bench appears divided or where there is a significant legal question on which GSTAT Benches across States are likely to take different views — invite the Bench to refer the matter to a larger Bench for a definitive ruling. This is the mechanism for establishing GSTAT precedent on unsettled GST issues (e.g., ITC reversals, place of supply, RCM applicability).
Rule 73 — Change of AR Requires Consent / Tribunal Permission
Goods and Services Tax Appellate Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 2025
Key Principle
A legal practitioner or authorised representative proposing to file a Vakalatnama... in any pending case... in which there is already a legal practitioner or authorised representative on record, shall do so only with the written consent of the legal practitioner or the authorised representative on record or when such consent is refused, with the permission of the Appellate Tribunal after revocation of Vakalatnama on an application filed in this behalf, which shall receive consideration only after service of such application on the counsel already on record.
When to Use — (Defensive): If a new CA/Advocate attempts to displace you as the AR on record without your NOC — oppose their vakalatnama at the registry level citing Rule 73. (For client): If your original AR refuses to give NOC — file formal application to Tribunal for permission. This prevents AR-hopping mid-proceedings that could disrupt case strategy.
Rule 106 — Recusal of Members (Conflict of Interest)
Goods and Services Tax Appellate Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 2025
Key Principle
For the purpose of maintaining the high standards and integrity of the Appellate Tribunal, the President or a Member of the Appellate Tribunal shall recuse himself — (a) in any case involving persons with whom the President or the Member has or had a personal, familial or professional relationship; (b) in any case concerning which the President or the Member has previously been called upon in another capacity; or (c) if there exist other circumstances such as to make the President or the Member's participation seem inappropriate.
When to Use — If you discover that a Member of the Bench previously appeared for the Department in the same matter, or has a financial interest in the issue, or was part of the same law firm — discreetly bring this to the Member's attention at the very first date. Do not wait. The Member must then voluntarily recuse. Note: parties cannot demand to know the reason for recusal.
Rule 120 — Exemplary Costs for Abuse of Process
Goods and Services Tax Appellate Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 2025
Key Principle
The Appellate Tribunal may in suitable cases direct appellant or respondent to bear the cost of litigation of the other side, and in case of abuse of process of court, impose exemplary costs on defaulting party.
When to Use — When Department: (a) files appeal only to harass the assessee knowing the legal position is against them, (b) repeatedly seeks adjournments and delays the matter, (c) does not comply with Tribunal orders — apply for exemplary costs under Rule 120(2). This is a powerful new tool at GSTAT which was not available at AAAR/AAR/Appellate Commissioner level.
Rule 103 — 30-Day Limit for Pronouncing Orders
Goods and Services Tax Appellate Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 2025
Key Principle
The Appellate Tribunal, after hearing the applicant and respondent, shall make and pronounce an order either at once or, as soon as thereafter as may be practicable but not later than thirty days from the final hearing excluding vacations or holidays.
When to Use — After final hearing, if 30 days pass without order — write formally to Registrar citing Rule 103, requesting expeditious pronouncement. This creates a record of your request and can support a mandamus petition to High Court if GSTAT repeatedly fails to pronounce orders. Particularly useful where pre-deposit money is locked and assessee is under financial pressure.
