Step 1 — Tax Liability & TDS
Advance Tax Obligation = Total Tax − TDS. TDS is a separate credit and is NOT an advance tax installment. Quarterly shortfall for 234C is tested on (Total Tax − TDS) × cumulative %. The 90% waiver applies only to Q4 (234C) and to 234B — not to Q1, Q2, Q3.

Critical Rule — Quarterly independence: Each quarter's shortfall is assessed independently on the date it falls due. Excess paid in Q3 or Q4 does NOT cancel interest already accrued in Q1 or Q2.
TDS is NOT advance tax: TDS does not count as a quarterly installment. It is a separate year-end credit. Quarterly obligation = (Total Tax − TDS) × %.
90% waiver — Q4 ONLY: If aggregate (Advance Tax + TDS) ≥ 90% of Total Tax, Q4 234C is waived. This rule does NOT apply to Q1/Q2/Q3.
Exception: Capital gains / casual income first earned after due date — proportionate 234C may be waived for that component.

Worked Example — verifying correct calculation
Given: Total Tax = ₹10,00,000  |  TDS = ₹2,00,000  |  Net Obligation = ₹8,00,000
Q1 (15 Jun): Required = 15% × ₹8,00,000 = ₹1,20,000. Paid = ₹2,00,000. Shortfall = ₹0. No 234C.
Q2 (15 Sep): Required = 45% × ₹8,00,000 = ₹3,60,000. Paid = ₹2,00,000. Shortfall = ₹1,60,000. 234C = ₹1,60,000 × 1% × 3 = ₹4,800.
Q3 (15 Dec): Required = 75% × ₹8,00,000 = ₹6,00,000. Paid = ₹6,50,000. Shortfall = ₹0. No 234C.
Q4 (15 Mar): Required = ₹8,00,000. Paid adv. = ₹8,00,000. Shortfall = ₹0. No 234C.
Total 234C = ₹4,800. The Q2 shortfall is charged even though Q3 & Q4 were fully paid on time.
