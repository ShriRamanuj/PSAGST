📝 Diplomacy = saying difficult things without breaking the bridge.

3 — The Behavioral Disease (Root Cause Identified) ⇧ Index
🕐 13:20 – 16:30 & 25:30 – 30:50 | The CORE diagnosis Papa gave
🦠 BEHAVIORAL DISEASE
Two Symptoms — One Root | Pre-existed since 11th standard
📝 Papa identified this after collecting 3-4 years of symptoms. This is the master problem — every other small issue is a side-effect of this one.
Two Points Dragging You Down (Co-Related)
SYMPTOM 1 — PRIMARY
📊 COMPARING WITH OTHERS
Compared with WHOM?
(i) Relatives
(ii) Classmates / Batchmates (Nikunj, Viraj)
(iii) Friends
"वो तो ये कर रहा है, मैं कुछ भी नहीं कर रहा"
(He's doing this, I'm doing nothing)
📝 Normal — everyone compares a bit. Disease — when it impacts your RESULTS (JEE marks, exam performance, mood).
💡 JEE attempt — "4-5 easy questions thay, maine hi galti ki" → mind wasn't stable BECAUSE comparison kept running in background.
SYMPTOM 2 — SIDE EFFECT
📉 UNDERESTIMATING OWN CALIBRE
"Mind को signal क्या जा रहा है?
'मुझे नहीं मिले, मुझे नहीं मिले'"
📝 Every time you say "I didn't get marks / I didn't make it", your subconscious mind takes it as instruction and underperforms next time too.
Result → Mind not stable → Mistakes in exams → Mediocre output despite real talent
💡 The Ankita-in-office example: multi-talented girl, but at the BOTTOM of her life because of THIS exact disease. Even now — sad at every wedding, says "मेरा talent waste हो गया".
⚠️ DANGER OF NOT FIXING
Day by day, this behaviour becomes pakka (cemented). It will go with you into marriage, job, every relationship. The person remains "दुखी से रहेगी, lifelong" even if outwardly successful.
✓ THE CURE — COMPARE ONLY WITH YOURSELF
"I am fighting against myself. The only one who can stop me is myself."

Compare with your own last year — last year's marks, last year's effort, last year's questions answered. Improve over THAT.
📝 This is the Atomic Habits principle — be 1% better than yesterday's you. In 1 year = 37x better. Compounding works in self-improvement just like in money.
💡 Papa's CA exam trick — wrote on his board: "My competitor is the PAPER, not my friends." Started thinking what questions paper will ask → topped because mind was focused on paper, not on friends' coaching reviews.
4 — Why People Demean You (No Knowledge Required) ⇧ Index
🕐 26:20 – 32:00 | Understanding the people who pull you down
🎭 The Universal Truth About Critics
Anyone — relative, classmate, neighbour — can demean you
Three Things That Need ZERO Knowledge
1
Pointing fingers at others
2
Comparing you with others
3
Showing ego in front of you
HARD TRUTH
"दूसरों से ego करने के लिए knowledge की गरज नहीं है।"
Anyone — A-grade or D-grade — can demean. It costs them nothing.
📝 So when uncle/aunty/cousin makes a comment — remember: their ability to comment doesn't mean their judgment is correct. A toddler can throw a stone at a Ferrari — the stone falling doesn't make the Ferrari bad.
Why They Do It — The Grade System
A-Grade (top) ⟶ B ⟶ C ⟶ D (bottom)

You are at A. They are at C or D.
They want to pull you down to THEIR grade — because pulling down is easier than climbing up.
📝 This is human psychology — crab mentality. When one crab tries to climb out of the bucket, others pull it back. Not because they hate the climber, but because seeing the climber rise makes them feel small.
📖 THE TWO SHIPS STORY (Papa's analogy)
Two ships passing each other. Between them, an iron grid.
Ship A: One bird flies into the grid, hits, gets a small bump → stops, learns, returns. Walks away alive.
Ship B: Another bird flies, hits, bleeds → but flies again, hits again, bleeds more → keeps hitting until its head is covered in blood.
📝 Provokers are the iron grid. If you keep banging your head against them (responding to every taunt), only YOU get hurt. If you stop after one hit (one observation), you walk away whole.
✓ THE STRATEGY
When you walk into a family function and find C/D-grade people gossiping —
Skip them. Go directly to the elder/aunty/uncle of your own caliber.
They will notice: "Yeh bachpan se mature hai, hum से बात कर रहा है."
💡 Papa's own practice — at family functions, he goes straight to talk with Krishn Gopal or Ashish bhaiya (his caliber). Doesn't waste time with gossipers. Result: industry-level discussions, real value, no draining.
5 — The Acceptance Principle (The Master Switch) ⇧ Index
🕐 10:37 – 12:30 & 55:40 – 58:25 | The mental switch that unlocks solutions
🔓 ACCEPT vs JUSTIFY
The choice that decides whether you grow or stagnate
The Katori (Bowl) Analogy
A katori facing UP → fills with knowledge from outside.
A katori facing DOWN → nothing enters, even if it rains wisdom.

"Justifying yourself" = katori facing down.
📝 The mind that says "yes but it wasn't my fault" stays empty. The mind that says "okay, where did I go wrong?" gets answers.
✗ WRONG PATH
JUSTIFY YOURSELF
"उनकी भी तो गलती है"
(They are also wrong)
"Justify किसी ने नहीं मिलता solution, सिर्फ reasons"
📝 Excuses give reasons. Reasons don't pay your bills or fix your problems.
✓ RIGHT PATH
ACCEPT YOUR GAP
"मेरी क्या कमी है, मैं उसमें focus करता हूँ"
Ask "HOW to do it?" (question mark)
NOT "WHY did this happen?" (full stop)
📝 Question mark = mind keeps searching for the answer = solution appears. Full stop = mind closes the case = no solution.
🔑 WHAT "SORRY" REALLY MEANS
Sorry ≠ guilt or weakness.
Sorry = "I have already changed my behaviour in the next second. I have already proven my quality."
After saying sorry, climbing up is no big deal — but you still have to score the runs.
📝 The acceptance is the easy part. The work after acceptance — that's where the runs get scored. Match ko jeetna toh padega hi.
PAPA'S OWN EXAMPLES OF ACCEPTANCE
"I accept my English is weak."
"I accept I started ticket-booking work."
"I accept I didn't move to Pune/Bombay for practice — that was my mistake."
"I accept I left Bombay."
"Now what can I do TODAY?" — that's the only useful question.
