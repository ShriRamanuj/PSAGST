Applied provisions

Income Tax
Income-tax Act, 2025 — section 62; Income-tax Rules, 2026 — rule 46(9)

Section 62 requires prescribed persons to keep books and other documents. Rule 46 lays down the maintenance rules, and rule 46(9) requires them to be kept for seven tax years from the end of the relevant tax year.

GST
CGST Act — section 36 read with section 44

Section 36 requires retention of books and records until expiry of 72 months from the due date of furnishing the annual return for the relevant year. If there is appeal, revision, other proceeding, or investigation, the later period applies.

Logic used in this tool
Plain computation model

Income Tax discard date = 31 March after seven completed tax years from the end of the FY. GST discard date = 72 months from the actual due date of annual return (GSTR-9) for that FY. For GST, the tool uses a year-wise due-date table for the years where Government extended the due date, and uses 31 December following the end of the FY where no separate extension has been built in. Common safe discard date = later of the two.

Important notes

  • This tool is for general compliance guidance and not a substitute for case-specific legal review.
  • Exemptions from filing annual return do not automatically mean records may be destroyed early.
  • If assessment, appeal, revision, search, investigation, or prosecution is involved, keep records longer.
  • The safest practical approach is to discard only when the later of both laws permits it.
